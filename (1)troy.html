Ten years have passed since the Greeks attacked Troy, and they were still there outside the stable walls, and fighting the locals proved to be a brave warrior. In the decade of the war, the sly Odysseus, the most trusted adviser and counsellor of the Greek leader King Agamemnon, planned to deceive the Trojan. He wanted to make the Greeks believe that they had lost their nerves and then returned to Greece. In the night, Greeks abandoned Troy and left only a giant wooden horse on the wheel outside the city's door. When the dawn fell, the Trojans were surprised to see that there were only horses, not Greek troops. They truly believed that the Greeks left this horse as a gift to the gods and had a good sea trip. So they drove wooden horses into the city and began revelation to celebrate the end of the war. However, unknown to the Trojan, Odysseus drilled a hole in a wooden horse to hide several Greek warriors. This plan was the only way to enter a city that had been in defence for many years. Now they were in Odysseus, and his men went out of the dummy and slaughtered unsuspecting guards. Then they opened the gates and forced the entire Greek army, hiding a few miles away, to enter the city. Therefore, thanks to Odysseus' plans, the Greeks won the Trojan War. At the end of the war, Odysseus and his men sailed to Ithaca, their hometown, but eventually, only one of them would return.
